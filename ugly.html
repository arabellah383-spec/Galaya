The Ugly Stepsister is a darkly comedic horror reimagining of the classic Cinderella tale. The story follows Elvira and Agnes, two stepsisters whose obsessive desire for beauty and status leads them down a twisted path of manipulation, jealousy, and supernatural terror. Set in a stylized, eerie world, the film blends grotesque horror with sharp humor, exploring themes of vanity, obsession, and the consequences of unchecked ambition. With shocking twists, unsettling imagery, and moments of dark satire, it offers a fresh, chilling take on a familiar fairy tale.

Review

The Ugly Stepsister delivers a bold and inventive twist on the classic Cinderella story, combining horror, dark comedy, and body‑horror elements in a visually striking package. The performances, especially from Lea Myren and Thea Sofie Loch Næss, bring depth and nuance to the otherwise grotesque and exaggerated characters. The film balances unsettling, grotesque imagery with sharp satirical humor, keeping viewers both horrified and entertained. While its story may not stray far from familiar fairy tale beats, the unique tone, creative direction, and unexpected twists make it a memorable and daring reimagining. Overall, it’s a thrilling, eerie, and darkly funny take on a classic tale for fans of horror-comedy and twisted fairy tales.
