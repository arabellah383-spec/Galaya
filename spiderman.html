The Amazing Spiderman

July 3, 2012 · Action · Adventure ·Science Fiction · Superhero · 2h 16m

6.9/10 ★

Cast:

Andrew Garfield, Emma Stone, Rhys Ifans, Denis Leary, Martin Sheen, Sally Field, Irrfan Khan, Campbell Scott, Embeth Davidtz, Chris Zylka, C. Thomas Howell, and Max Charles — with Andrew Garfield as Spider-Man / Peter Parker.

The Amazing Spider-Man follows teenager Peter Parker, a socially awkward student who discovers a mysterious briefcase belonging to his late father. Seeking answers about his parents' past, Peter uncovers a secret experiment and gains incredible spider-like abilities. As he struggles to balance his new identity with his normal life and growing feelings for Gwen Stacy, Peter faces a dangerous new enemy, Dr. Curt Connors, whose transformation into the Lizard threatens the city. Peter must embrace his destiny as Spider-Man and protect the people he loves.

Review

The Amazing Spider-Man offers a fresh and emotional take on the classic Spider-Man story. Andrew Garfield delivers a more realistic and heartfelt performance as Peter Parker, showing a believable mix of awkwardness, humor, and pain. His chemistry with Emma Stone, who plays Gwen Stacy, adds genuine warmth and charm to the film. The movie focuses more on Peter’s personal struggles and relationships, giving it a deeper and more grounded tone compared to earlier versions. The action scenes are exciting and visually stunning, especially Spider-Man’s web-slinging sequences across New York. Rhys Ifans brings complexity to Dr. Curt Connors, making the Lizard both threatening and tragic. While some might feel the story retells familiar elements, the film still succeeds in delivering a compelling origin story with strong acting, emotional depth, and thrilling action. Overall, The Amazing Spider-Man stands out as a fresh and engaging superhero film that brings a modern touch to a beloved character.
